Projects

  • Website for Carnegie Mellon Quiz Bowl
    During the summer prior to the 2018-2019 school year, I built a new website for Carnegie Mellon Quiz Bowl, an organization that plays and hosts academic knowledge competitions. I used the materialize React framework to streamline the development process, which only took about 28 hours total. Before this, I never used HTML, CSS, JS, or React before; By developing the website and heavily modifying the react framework, I learned all of these skills on-the fly, while producing a presentable product in a relatively short amount of time.
  • Brain-Computer-Interfaces, Capital Area Science and Engineering Fair
    During my senior year of high school, I engaged in a massive foray into Machine Learning and Digital Signal Processing for the Capital Area Science and Engineering Fair in Harrisburg, PA, where I finished 2nd in my category. My goal was to determine what a human subject was doing using their electroencephalogram (EEG) signals out of 4 classes. I built my own EEG headset using mostly common hardware and a 3D printer. From there, I gleaned features out of the EEG signals throguh Signal Processing and feature analysis. I also implemented several Bayesian Machine Learning algorithms to do the actual classification.

  • PaupackWaves Lemelson-MIT InvenTeam
    In 9th grade, I was a member of a team at my High School that recieved a $10,000 grant from the Lemelson-MIT foundation to build an invention. It was an electric generator that drew enough power from the small, choppy waves of Lake Wallenpaupack to light the docks at night. I designed and built most of the electronic systems, designing the PCB and programming the PIC microcontroller present on the board.

  • Geo Beats Audio-Visualization Game
    For my term project in 15-112, an introductory Computer Science class at Carnegie Mellon University, I used Python to create a Music Visualization game in the guise of a "bullet-hell", Galaga-style srolling shooter.
